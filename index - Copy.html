I am an assistant professor at TU Delft since Sep. 2016. Before this, I was a HC Ørsted postdoc fellow at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, DTU Denmark. I obtained my PhD in Computer Science in 2015 from TU Munich, and a PhD in Mechanical Engineering in 2012 from Beihang University.


I develop computational design and fabrication methods to facilitate the design for the best performance, exploiting the unprecedented design freedom offered by additive manufacturing (3D printing). Exemplary topics include:

 

(1) Design of medical devices with tailor-made physical properties,

(2) Design of soft-bodied robots for stable physical interaction,

(3) Design of lightweight and robust structures for 3D printing.

 

Underlying such diverse design problems is computer simulation and optimization (e.g., topology optimization) of shapes, structures, and (meta-)materials. It builds upon and extends the state-of-the-art in computer graphics, computer-aided design, and computational mechanics, and in particular regarding geometry and physics.


I'm privileged to work with talented PhD students and postdocs: Kai Wu (10.2020-), Xiaoya Zhai (9.2019-), Eric Garner (10.2018-), Tim Kuipers (9.2017-), Rob Scharff (1.2017-), Dr. Weiming Wang (4.2018-5.2020), Jeroen Groen (3-4.2018).
